Browning tips on my Calathea Ornata - overwatering or lack of pure water? ⚠️

Hey everyone, I’m running into issues with my Calathea Ornata. My integrated sensor logs show constant soil hydration levels around 78%, which seems stable, but the lower margins of the leaves are crisping up dramatically.

Could this be mineral shock from using local tap water, or is my ambient household humidity (currently fluctuating near 45%) simply too low for this delicate foliage? I've attached my live IoT telemetry widget below for reference. Let me know what you think!

David, you hit the nail on the head. Calatheas are notorious for being fluoride and chlorine sensitive. Tap water chemical buildups manifest as crisping margins. Additionally, 45% ambient humidity is simply too low for Calathea Ornata—they thrive in 60%+ ranges. Swap to distilled water or rainwater, and run a dedicated humidifier in that zone!
